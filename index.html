Understanding the Supply Chain

What "Canadian" actually means.

The manufacturing of a research peptide is a multi-stage process. Each stage can legally occur in a different country, and in this industry, it often does. What matters is understanding which stages are happening where, and whether those stages have been properly verified.

1

Synthesis

The peptide molecule is chemically built, amino acid by amino acid. This is the most consequential stage for purity and quality.

Most critical
2

QC Testing

The manufacturer tests their own batch and issues an internal Certificate of Analysis confirming identity and purity.

3

Lyophilization

The peptide is freeze-dried into stable powder form for storage and shipping. This can occur at the synthesis facility or elsewhere.

4

Packaging

Vials are filled, sealed, and labelled. This is frequently the stage that occurs domestically in Canada.

5

Distribution

The finished product is shipped to the customer from a domestic address or warehouse.

A company that packages or ships from Canada is technically a Canadian company. That is accurate. It does not, however, tell you anything about where the peptide itself was synthesized, by whom, or under what conditions.

The vast majority of research peptides sold globally, including by well-regarded Canadian companies, are synthesized in China. That is not a criticism of any company. China is home to some of the most advanced pharmaceutical synthesis infrastructure in the world. The relevant question is never simply where something is made. The relevant question is whether anyone has personally verified that it was made to a rigorous standard.

Lower-priced products that market themselves as "Canadian" often reflect a shorter supply chain: bulk material imported from an unverified overseas source and repackaged domestically. That model is not inherently problematic, but it leaves meaningful gaps in quality verification and leaves customers without the full picture of what they are buying.

Our position: We are not claiming our supply chain is more Canadian than others. We are claiming it is more transparent, more thoroughly verified, and more traceable. That is the standard we hold ourselves to.

We Went There

We flew to Guangzhou.
We walked the floors.

Sourcing peptides from a manufacturer you have never visited is a decision most companies in this industry make without a second thought. We made a different one.

Guangzhou is one of the world's primary hubs for pharmaceutical-grade peptide synthesis. Our team and the founder of PureTide travelled there to tour the manufacturing facilities that supply our products, meeting the people responsible for synthesis, reviewing their equipment, and observing their quality control processes in person.

Gaining access was not straightforward. Out of dozens of manufacturers contacted, only a handful were willing to allow facility visits. Most synthetic peptide producers do not open their doors to outside parties. That reluctance is itself informative. The facilities willing to be seen are the ones with nothing to conceal.

The photos and video below are from that visit. We are sharing them so you can see exactly where our products come from, and so you know that someone from PureTide has stood in that facility and made an informed judgment about what happens there.

Our Testing Process

Verification that goes
beyond the manufacturer.

A manufacturer's Certificate of Analysis is issued by the same party with a financial interest in selling the product. That is a structural conflict of interest, and we do not treat it as sufficient on its own.

01

Manufacturer COA

Our synthesis partners test each batch internally and issue their own Certificate of Analysis confirming compound identity and purity levels.

02

Independent Retesting
by Testides

We select batches for independent retesting and submit them to Testides, a Canadian analytical laboratory with no financial relationship to our suppliers. This is the verification that matters.

03

COA Published
on Product Page

The verified COA is posted publicly on every PureTide product page before purchase. Full results. No request required. Every batch.

Why Random Batch Testing?

Quality can vary between batches even within the same synthesis facility. Testing only an initial batch and treating subsequent runs as equivalent is not a rigorous standard. Our randomized approach means any batch you receive could have been the one independently verified by Testides, which holds our supply chain to a consistent and unpredictable accountability standard.

What Testides Verifies

Testides is a Canadian analytical laboratory that confirms peptide identity, purity levels, and the absence of contaminants. Their work is conducted independently of PureTide and independently of our manufacturers. The results they produce are what appear on your COA, published in full on every product page prior to purchase.
